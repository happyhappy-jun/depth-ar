\caption{\textbf{The same method, the same layers, two opposite verdicts.} Quality
against the fraction of blocks skipped, for Qwen2.5-0.5B (circles), Qwen2.5-1.5B
(squares) and Qwen2.5-7B (triangles), with layers chosen by measured residual damage and every predictor skipping
the identical blocks. \emph{Left:} on WikiText-2 likelihood, \mname (solid) sits below
plain skipping (dashed) at every budget and both scales --- it recovers a real part of
what skipping destroys, and the scalar predictor (dotted) recovers less. \emph{Right:}
on downstream accuracy, the same curves at the same layer sets fall \emph{on or below}
plain skipping. The insets plot the difference against plain skipping directly: negative
is an improvement on the left and a regression on the right, and \mname is negative in
both. The two panels are not a strong result and a weak one; they are a repair measured
in one currency and its absence in another. Recovering a skipped layer's contribution to
the loss is not recovering its contribution to the model's behaviour. 0.5B in FP32 on an
NVIDIA TITAN X (Pascal) GPU; 1.5B in BF16 on a single NVIDIA RTX 3090.}
\label{fig:dissociation}

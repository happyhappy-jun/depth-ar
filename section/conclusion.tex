\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Plain layer skipping assumes a skipped block's residual update is zero. It is not: much of
it is predictable from the updates that precede it, and \mname recovers a substantial share
of the likelihood that skipping destroys, in closed form, at \phm{0.64}\% of prefill
latency. At a matched budget of four skipped blocks the recovery grows with scale, it holds
under every selection rule we tried, and under aggressive pruning it converts into a large,
statistically reliable downstream gain over plain skipping (at Qwen2.5-1.5B; the pruned
models remain below dense quality).

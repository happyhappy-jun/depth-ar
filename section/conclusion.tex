\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Plain layer skipping treats a missing residual update as zero; \mname treats it
as predictable. Fitting one or two scalars per skipped block against the
preceding depth trajectory --- in closed form, on unlabeled text, with no gradient
training --- recovers \ph{62}\% of the quality that skipping discards, at $O(Td)$
cost. Transformer depth carries momentum, and plain skipping throws it away.

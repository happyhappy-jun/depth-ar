\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Plain layer skipping treats a missing residual update as zero; \mname treats it as
predictable, and fits the prediction in closed form on unlabeled text. Two things
had to be got right. The predictor must be per-channel --- a single coefficient per
layer is, at some layers, worse than dropping the update outright. And the layers
must be chosen by measured recovery, not by predictability: how well an update can
be forecast tells you almost nothing about whether forecasting it helps. Depth
carries momentum only in its last quarter; before that, blocks partially undo one
another. Skipping discards computation the model has already forecast --- but
forecasting it accurately and getting it back are not the same thing.

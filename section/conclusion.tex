\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Plain layer skipping assumes a skipped block's residual update is zero. It is not: much of it
is predictable from the updates that precede it, and \mname recovers a substantial share of
the likelihood that skipping destroys, in closed form, at 0.64\% of prefill latency.
Across the Qwen2.5 family, the likelihood it recovers scales with the damage skipping
inflicts, and significant downstream gains appear only where that damage is severe. Where plain
skipping barely hurts, the accuracy effects lie below the evaluation's own noise floor; where
it hurts badly, \mname recovers a large and statistically reliable share of what was lost,
including under the deployable selection rule at scale. Repair pays off where pruning bites, and a first test outside that family bounds the ordering rather than
confirming it (\Cref{sec:app-qwen3}).
